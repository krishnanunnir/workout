# Daily A/B — Bodyweight Foundation

A very simple beginner plan focused on consistency and controlled effort.

- **Frequency:** every day
- **Equipment:** bodyweight only; chair/wall/table optional
- **Cardio:** not included; cardio is handled separately
- **Structure:** alternate Workout A and Workout B
- **Main target:** finish the daily work at the right effort, not within a fixed time
- **Goal:** build the habit, improve basic strength, and avoid overthinking

## Daily structure

Every day uses the same simple structure:

1. **Warm up briefly** — enough to feel ready.
2. **Do today's workout** — A or B, 3 rounds.
3. **Cool down if needed** — stretch/breathe enough to feel normal again.

There is no required 30-minute constraint. Some days may be quicker; some days may need more rest. The important thing is the effort level.

## Schedule

Alternate A/B every day:

| Day | Workout |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | A — lower body + core |
| Day 2 | B — upper body + posture/core |
| Day 3 | A |
| Day 4 | B |
| Day 5 | A |
| Day 6 | B |
| Repeat | Keep alternating |

## Warm-up

Do this before the workout:

| Exercise | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Arm circles | 30 sec |
| Hip circles | 30 sec |
| Cat-cow | 8 reps |
| Easy squats | 10 reps |
| Good mornings | 10 reps |

## Workout A — lower body + core

Do **3 rounds**. Move slowly. Rest as needed.

| Exercise | Reps |
|---|---:|
| Chair squat | 10 |
| Glute bridge | 12 |
| Reverse lunge or supported split squat | 6/side |
| Dead bug | 8/side |

## Workout B — upper body + posture/core

Do **3 rounds**. Move slowly. Rest as needed.

| Exercise | Reps/time |
|---|---:|
| Incline push-up | 8 |
| Reverse snow angel | 10 |
| Bird dog | 8/side |
| Plank | 20–30 sec |

If you later get a band/table setup, reverse snow angels can become band rows or table rows.

## Cooldown

Optional, but useful if you feel tight or rushed after the workout:

| Exercise | Time |
|---|---:|
| Child’s pose | 30–60 sec |
| Hip flexor stretch | 30–60 sec/side |
| Chest/shoulder stretch | 30–60 sec |
| Easy breathing | 30–60 sec |

## Effort rule

This is the main rule.

Most days should feel like **5–6 out of 10**.

Finish thinking:

> I could do more, but I am stopping here.

Avoid finishing destroyed. Avoid daily 8–9/10 effort.

## How to adjust effort

If the workout feels too easy:

1. Make reps slower and cleaner.
2. Add reps according to the weekly progression.
3. Use a slightly harder variation later.
4. Shorten rest slightly only if it still does not feel like cardio.

If the workout feels too hard:

1. Rest longer.
2. Use an easier variation.
3. Keep the same reps for another week.
4. Do 2 rounds instead of 3 for that day only.

Consistency matters more than forcing the prescription.

## 4-week rep progression

If a week feels too hard, repeat that week instead of progressing.

### Workout A — lower body + core

| Week | Rounds | Chair squat | Glute bridge | Reverse lunge / split squat | Dead bug |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Week 1 | 3 | 10 | 12 | 6/side | 8/side |
| Week 2 | 3 | 12 | 14 | 7/side | 9/side |
| Week 3 | 3 | 14 | 16 | 8/side | 10/side |
| Week 4 | 3 | 15 | 18 | 9/side | 11/side |

### Workout B — upper body + posture/core

| Week | Rounds | Incline push-up | Reverse snow angel | Bird dog | Plank |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Week 1 | 3 | 8 | 10 | 8/side | 20–30 sec |
| Week 2 | 3 | 9 | 12 | 9/side | 25–30 sec |
| Week 3 | 3 | 10 | 14 | 10/side | 30–35 sec |
| Week 4 | 3 | 11 | 15 | 11/side | 35–40 sec |

## Progress only if

- No joint pain
- Soreness is mild
- You can still do the workout daily
- Effort is below 6/10
- Form is controlled

If the daily habit starts to feel threatened, reduce intensity first. Consistency is the main goal.
