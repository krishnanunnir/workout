Workout A

Bodyweight squat

Sit your hips back and down, then stand tall. Use a chair as a target only if you need it to keep the movement clean.

  • Feet about shoulder-width.
  • Knees track in the same direction as toes.
  • Stand by pushing the floor away.
  • Easier fallback: chair squat.

Avoid: collapsing knees inward, dropping fast, or bouncing at the bottom.

Workout A

Pause glute bridge

Lie on your back, knees bent, feet planted. Lift hips until your body forms a clean line from shoulders to knees, then pause for about 2 seconds.

  • Push through heels and mid-foot.
  • Squeeze glutes at the top for a short pause.
  • Keep ribs down; do not over-arch the lower back.

Avoid: turning it into a lower-back arch instead of a hip lift.

Workout A

Reverse lunge

Step one leg back and lower gently, then return to standing. Use support only if balance makes reps messy.

  • Keep the front foot planted.
  • Lower only as far as control allows.
  • Use support only when needed; clean reps matter most.

Avoid: pushing through knee pain or wobbling through ugly reps. Easier fallback: supported split squat.

Workout A

Slow dead bug

Lie on your back with arms up and knees bent. Move opposite arm and leg away slowly while your trunk stays quiet. The slowness is the exercise.

  • Lower back stays gently connected to the floor.
  • Move slowly; pause before switching sides.
  • Make the range smaller if your back arches.

Avoid: rushing or letting the ribs flare up.

Workout B

Incline push-up

Place hands on a wall, counter, table, or bench. Keep your body in one line as you lower and press back up.

  • Hands slightly wider than shoulders.
  • Body moves as one piece.
  • Higher surface = easier; lower surface = harder.

Avoid: sagging hips, shrugging shoulders, or flaring elbows aggressively.

Workout B

Pause reverse snow angel

Lie face down. Lift hands slightly, sweep arms in a wide arc, and pause briefly in the hard positions.

  • Keep neck long; look down.
  • Move from the shoulder blades.
  • Small range is fine.
  • Pause for 1–2 seconds where you can control it.

Avoid: cranking the neck up or forcing the shoulders through pinching.

Workout B

Pause bird dog

Start on hands and knees. Extend opposite arm and leg, pause briefly, then return without twisting.

  • Reach long, not high.
  • Pause for 2 seconds at full extension.
  • Keep hips square to the floor.

Avoid: twisting open or arching the lower back.

Workout B

Full plank

Hold a straight, braced position from shoulders to ankles. Use knees-down plank only as the easier fallback if effort climbs too high.

  • Push elbows or hands into the floor.
  • Squeeze glutes lightly.
  • Breathe quietly; stop before form sags.

Avoid: holding past clean form just to win seconds.
