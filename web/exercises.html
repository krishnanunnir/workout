movement guide

Harder, still repeatable.

These are the upgraded bodyweight movements. If effort jumps past 6/10, cut reps first or use the fallback.

Workout A

Pause bodyweight squat

Squat down under control, pause briefly at the bottom, then stand tall. The pause makes the basic squat more honest.

  • Feet about shoulder-width.
  • Pause for about 1 second where you can stay controlled.
  • Stand by pushing the floor away.
  • Easier fallback: regular bodyweight squat or chair squat.

Avoid: bouncing out of the bottom or letting knees collapse inward.

Workout A

Single-leg glute bridge

Bridge with one foot planted and the other leg lifted. Keep hips level and lift from the working glute.

  • Push through the planted heel and mid-foot.
  • Keep hips square; do not twist upward.
  • Pause briefly at the top if reps are clean.
  • Easier fallback: pause glute bridge or regular bridge.

Avoid: arching the lower back or rotating the hips to cheat the rep.

Workout A

Reverse lunge

Step one leg back and lower gently, then return to standing. Use support only if balance makes reps messy.

  • Keep the front foot planted.
  • Lower only as far as control allows.
  • Use support only when needed; clean reps matter most.

Avoid: pushing through knee pain or wobbling through ugly reps.

Workout A

Long-lever dead bug

Move opposite arm and leg away slowly, reaching longer than a normal dead bug while keeping your trunk quiet.

  • Lower back stays controlled.
  • Reach long, then return slowly.
  • Make the range smaller if your back arches.
  • Easier fallback: slow dead bug or short-range dead bug.

Avoid: chasing range after your ribs or lower back lose position.

Workout B

Incline push-up

Place hands on a wall, counter, table, or bench. Keep your body in one line as you lower and press back up.

  • Hands slightly wider than shoulders.
  • Body moves as one piece.
  • Higher surface = easier; lower surface = harder.

Avoid: sagging hips, shrugged shoulders, or flared elbows.

Workout B

Prone W raise

Lie face down with elbows bent like a W. Lift hands and elbows slightly, squeeze shoulder blades, then lower with control.

  • Look down; keep neck long.
  • Think shoulder blades back and down.
  • Pause briefly at the top.
  • Easier fallback: pause reverse snow angel.

Avoid: shrugging toward the ears or forcing through shoulder pinching.

Workout B

Bird-dog crunch

Extend opposite arm and leg, then bring elbow and knee toward each other without letting your hips twist.

  • Reach long first.
  • Move slowly into the crunch.
  • Keep hips square to the floor.
  • Easier fallback: pause bird dog.

Avoid: twisting open or rushing the elbow-to-knee part.

Workout B

Plank shoulder taps

Hold a plank and slowly tap the opposite shoulder. The goal is to stop your hips from rocking.

  • Set feet wider if needed.
  • Tap slowly; no rushing.
  • Keep ribs down and glutes lightly squeezed.
  • Easier fallback: full plank or knees plank.

Avoid: rocking side to side just to finish reps.
